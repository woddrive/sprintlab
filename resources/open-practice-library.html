Open Practice Library (OPL)

What is the OPL?

The OPL is a freely accessible online resource that offers a collection of problem-solving practices and guidance for product development (including Design Sprints!), project management, and team collaboration. The OPL was originally created by Red Hat as internal training resource and was eventually made public as an open source project. The OPL was inspired by the Agile Manifesto, which emphasizes collaboration, customer satisfaction, and responding to change.

The OPL website provides an extensive library of practices that cover a wide range of topics and methodologies, such as Agile, Lean, DevOps, Design Thinking, and more. Each practice consists of a description, key principles, and step-by-step guidance on how to implement it. Users can search for practices by category or keyword, and they can contribute their own practices or feedback.

The OPL has several goals that are aligned with its mission of promoting knowledge sharing, process improvement, and collaboration:

  1. Facilitate knowledge sharing: The OPL aims to create a community of practitioners who can share their experiences, insights, and best practices with others. By providing a platform for knowledge sharing, the OPL enables individuals and organizations to learn from each other and to avoid reinventing the wheel.
  2. Foster process improvement: The OPL offers a collection of best practices and guidance that can help individuals and o rganizations improve their product development, project management, and team collaboration processes. By following proven practices and adapting them to their specific needs, users can enhance their efficiency, quality, and customer satisfaction.
  3. Encourage collaboration: The OPL emphasizes the importance of collaboration among different teams and stakeholders, such as product owners, developers, testers, and customers. By promoting practices that foster collaboration, such as Agile, Lean, and Design Thinking, the OPL enables users to work together more effectively and to deliver value faster.
Who Benefits from the OPL?
Leadership and Product Stakeholders

OPL strategies provide a set of proven methodologies designed to improve team alignment, enhance communication, and increase project success rates. Use the OPL to foster a culture of continuous learning and improvement, resulting in higher product quality, faster time-to-market, and improved return on investment.

Product Owners and Managers

Take charge of your projects with valuable strategies to better manage project deliverables, streamline decision-making processes, and align team efforts with business goals. Learn to optimize your backlog, improve your planning and forecasting, and cultivate a customer-focused development process.

Software Developers

Level up with best practices to enhance your coding efficiency, improve collaboration, and optimize workflows. Practical techniques guide you in delivering high-quality software solutions in a time-effective manner. Improve problem-solving skills, foster better communication with your team, and elevate your development process.

Connecting OPL Practices with the Design Sprint Framework

The OPL is a treasure trove of proven practices designed to streamline the product development process. When it comes to Design Sprints, the OPL is especially invaluable, offering detailed guidance on specific practices integral to this powerful framework.

Recall that a Design Sprint involves a structured, five-day process used to quickly design, prototype, and test solutions to challenging problems. The OPL's catalog of practices provides in-depth insight into each step of the Design Sprint, from the initial problem mapping and sketching of solutions, to the decisive selection process, prototyping, and testing.

For instance, during the first (Understand) phase of a Design Sprint, the OPL recommends practices such as Event Storming, where the team visually maps out a process to gain a shared understanding. Another practice, User Story Mapping, helps in prioritizing tasks based on the user's perspective, aiding teams in maintaining a user-centric approach throughout the sprint. Other OPL practices, such as Start at the End and How Might We are also valuable exercises that enable sprint teams to gain understanding and alignment on the problem space.

As the sprint team starts to brainstorm and decide on potential solutions, techniques such as Lightning Talks, Dot Voting, and Storyboarding come into play. These are practices that encourage creative ideation and collaborative decision-making. For the Prototype phase, the OPL suggests techniques like Prototyping, a practice of creating simplified versions of a product to test its premise before full-scale development.

In the final phase of the Design Sprint, Validate, the OPL encourages practices such as Usability Testing to gain valuable user feedback. This feedback informs decision-making, helping teams understand if they are on the right track or need to pivot.

These are just a few examples of the many OPL practices that are available to optimize each phase of a Design Sprint. By providing a wide array of practices along with detailed guidance on their implementation, the Open Practice Library empowers teams to maximize the effectiveness of their Design Sprints and ultimately, deliver more impactful and successful products.
