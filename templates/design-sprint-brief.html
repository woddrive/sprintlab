Design Sprint Brief

Guiding the Design Sprint Journey

The sprint brief plays a pivotal role in the design sprint journey, serving as a compass that guides the team throughout the entire process. It is a crucial document that captures the essential information, goals, and challenges to be addressed during the sprint. The sprint brief provides clarity and alignment, ensuring that everyone involved has a shared understanding of the sprint's purpose, objectives, and desired outcomes. By establishing a solid foundation from the start, the sprint brief sets the stage for productive collaboration, focused ideation, and effective decision-making. It serves as a reference point, keeping the team on track and enabling them to channel their energy towards creating innovative solutions. With a well-crafted sprint brief, the design sprint journey becomes a purposeful and structured adventure, leading to meaningful results.

Sprint Brief Template

Adapt these sections to fit the specific context of your project.

COMPANY/PROJECT NAME
Design Sprint Brief
  1. Sprint Goals
    • Clearly define the goals and objectives of the design sprint.
    • Outline what you aim to achieve and the desired outcomes.
  2. Problem Statement
    • Provide a concise statement that encapsulates the problem or challenge to be addressed.
    • Clearly articulate the pain points and areas for improvement.
  3. Key Stakeholders
    • Identify the main stakeholders involved in the design sprint.
    • Specify their roles and responsibilities within the sprint process.
  4. Sprint Timeline
    • Outline the duration of the sprint, including specific dates for each day's activities.
    • Communicate the expected time commitment from participants.
  5. User Insights
    • Summarize any existing research or insights that inform the sprint (see Research Tasks).
    • Provide relevant background information about the target users.
  6. Deliverables
    • Specify the expected deliverables at the end of the sprint.
    • Outline the tangible outcomes, such as prototypes, user feedback, or validation results.
  7. Design Challenges
    • Highlight the key design challenges or areas that need focus during the sprint.
    • Identify any constraints or limitations to be considered.
  8. Design Sprint Team
    • List the names and roles of the team members participating in the sprint.
    • Clearly define the expertise and skills they bring to the sprint.
  9. Success Criteria
    • Define the metrics or criteria that will determine the success of the design sprint.
    • Specify the indicators that will gauge the achievement of goals.
  10. Additional Notes
    • Include any additional information, considerations, or specific instructions relevant to the design sprint.
