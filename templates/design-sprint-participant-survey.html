Design Sprint Participant Survey

Gathering Valuable Insights

The Design Sprint Participant Survey serves as a crucial tool in capturing participant expectations, goals, and desired outcomes, providing the facilitator with valuable information to tailor the sprint experience accordingly. By leveraging this survey, you'll gather the necessary insights to create a customized and effective design sprint that addresses participant needs and maximizes the potential for success.

Survey Questions

Here is a sample of ten survey questions that can capture relevant information about the participants' expectations and desired outcomes for the design sprint:

  1. What is your role within the organization and how does it relate to the design sprint?
  2. Have you participated in a design sprint before? If yes, please briefly describe your previous experience.
  3. What are your primary goals and expectations for this design sprint?
  4. What specific outcomes or deliverables would you like to see at the end of the design sprint?
  5. How do you envision your contribution to the design sprint process? What expertise or skills will you bring to the table?
  6. Are there any particular challenges or pain points that you hope to address through this design sprint?
  7. How do you think the design sprint will benefit the overall product development process and the end-users?
  8. Do you have any specific ideas or concepts that you would like to explore during the design sprint? If yes, please briefly describe them.
  9. What are your expectations regarding the collaboration and communication within the design sprint team?
  10. How would you measure the success of this design sprint? What key indicators or outcomes would you consider as markers of success?

These survey questions will help the facilitator gather valuable insights about the participants' expectations, goals, expertise, and desired outcomes for the design sprint.
